ACOA License

The Aggrandised, Convalesced, Open Access License, or ACOA License, is a license dedicated to upholding free access, through forbidding the restriction of access by:

  1. Putting the software behind a paywall or by requiring compensation,
  2. Sublicensing the Software to a more restrictive license,
  3. Purposefully increasing the difficulty of obtaining copies of the software, or by
  4. Any other means.

The new ACOA2 License offers some much-needed exceptions to the anti-restriction clause, and should be used over the ACOA1 license.

If you use only the ACOA license, your software and its derivatives should remain free of charge to everyone.

Others are free to use, copy, modify, merge, publish, and/or distribute copies of your software if they follow the conditions outlined in the license.

You can include mutliple licenses so that anyone who is creating a derivative can choose which license to use in their project. This allows more flexibility than a copyleft license like GPL.

You can re-license software licensed under ACOA licenses to any of their succesors freely. This means that if you were to use the ACOA2 license today, you can just switch to the ACOA3 license when it is released, and for anyone who adopted the ACOA1 license, you can now switch to the ACOA2 license.

It contains the same warranty and liability disclaimer as the MIT license, so you will get the same protections as if you had used the MIT license.
