ACOA licence

The Aggrandised, Convalescent, Open Access licence, or ACOA licence, is a licence dedicated to upholding free access, through forbidding the restriction of access by:

  1. Putting the software behind a paywall or by requiring compensation,
  2. Sublicensing the Software to a more restrictive licence,
  3. Purposefully increasing the difficulty of obtaining copies of the software, or by
  4. Any other means.
If you use only the ACOA licence, your software and its derivatives should remain free of charge to everyone.

Others are free to use, copy, modify, merge, publish, and/or distribute copies of your software if they follow the conditions outlined in the licence.

You can include mutliple licences so that anyone who is creating a derivative can choose which licence to use in their project. This allows more flexibility than a copyleft licence like GPL.

You can re-licence software licenced under ACOA licences to any of their succesors freely. This means that if you were to use the ACOA3 licence today, you can just switch to the ACOA4 licence when it is released, and for anyone who adopted the ACOA1/2 licence, you can now switch to the ACOA3 licence.

It contains the same warranty and liability disclaimer as the MIT licence, so you will get the same protections as if you had used the MIT licence.
